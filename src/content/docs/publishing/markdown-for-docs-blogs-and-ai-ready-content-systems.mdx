## Quick answer

Markdown is valuable in the AI era because it is light enough to edit, structured enough to reuse, and portable enough to republish.

It works especially well when it becomes the canonical content layer underneath:

- documentation,
- blogs,
- notes,
- image derivatives,
- and AI-assisted summarization or reuse.

## Where teams go wrong

Teams lose the benefit of Markdown when they let every destination own its own copy. That creates docs in one system, blog posts in another, social snippets in another, and AI prompts disconnected from the original source.

## Better operating model

Use Markdown as the source. Then derive:

- HTML docs,
- blog pages,
- newsletters,
- long images via the tool,
- and AI-assisted summaries from that source.
